3.5. Specifying SequencesÂ¶

As discussed in Section 3.4, the input sequence to a HOF is specified via a sequence(...) clause, where the ellipsis is replaced by a sequence specification. One of the specifications provided is "good_hits"_in("spill"), which indicates that a sequence should be formed from data products labeled "good_hits" from each "spill" data-set category.

A sequence specification contains one or more product specifications and zero or more resource specifications, expressed in EBNF grammar [Wiki-EBNF] as:

<sequence_spec> ::= product_specs | products "," resource_specs
product_specs   ::= Product | Product "," (product_specs | resource_specs)
resource_specs  ::= Resource | Resource "," resource_specs
products        ::= Product | Product "," products

where Product and Resource specify a single product and a single resource, respectively.

In the code examples that follow, the term <sequence_spec> refers to any specification allowed in the sequence(...) clause. The specifications for Product and Resource depend on the computing language of the algorithm and are defined elsewhere.
